## Foxconn 85% automation
- Status: outdated
- Findings: The 85% figure is **not a company-wide target** — it is an **achieved automation level specific to Foxconn's battery plants in Taiwan** (Kaohsiung and Hefa), reported in November 2025. The claim "across all manufacturing" is incorrect and misattributes a single-facility achievement. Foxconn's broader automation targets (e.g., 30% by 2020 for Chinese factories, per 2022 reports) are much lower and facility-specific. There is no credible source stating Foxconn targets 85% automation across **all** its manufacturing globally.
- Source: https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=4473 (CommonWealth Magazine, 2025-11-25) — accessed 2025

## Siemens Amberg 15 defects per million
- Status: outdated
- Findings: The "15 defects per million" figure originated from a **Gartner Industry Research study conducted in 2010** on the Siemens Amberg plant. It was widely cited in industry blogs and articles from 2014–2019. However, Siemens' own official fact sheets (2015), presentations (2016–2017), and recent academic/industry sources (2025–2026) consistently report **11 DPM (or 11.5 PPM)**, equivalent to 99.99885% quality. The defect rate improved from 500 DPM in 1989 → ~15 DPM (2010 Gartner) → ~11 DPM (current). The 15 DPM figure is **not current as of 2026**.
- Source: Official Siemens fact sheet (2015-02-23): https://assets.new.siemens.com/siemens/assets/api/uuid:6b67972d-4992-4cd0-b202-8a698fae046a/factsheet-amberg-en.pdf — accessed 2025; Gartner 2010 study citations: https://www.processindustryforum.com/hot-topics/internet-of-things-brings-new-industrial-revolution-factory-floor (2014-05-26) — accessed 2025; Recent 11 DPM data: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/isj.70006 (2025-08-05) — accessed 2025

## Tesla Berlin humans on final assembly
- Status: verified
- Findings: As of October 2025 (video factory tour) and early 2026, **Tesla Berlin still has substantial human workforce on final/general assembly**. The NextBigFuture video tour documents GA with ~1,000 employees per shift on 7 main lines installing wheels, seats, doors, windows, and connecting powertrain. Tesla's own manufacturing language states "robots complement the manual precision of human workers." While Musk has teased Optimus humanoid robots and Cybercab production for Berlin (Feb-Mar 2026), these are **future plans** — not deployed on the current Model Y final assembly line. Tesla's Q1 2026 update shows Berlin focused on Model Y production (>375K/yr capacity) with no indication that final assembly is fully automated. No April 2026 source reports removal of humans from final assembly.
